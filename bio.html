Biography

I completed my undergraduate education in the dual degree program at Tufts University and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston (SMFA) in August 2019, with a triple major: a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and French at Tufts, and a Bachelor of Fine Arts, with a focus in drawing and printmaking, at the SMFA.

I am bilingual in English and French, and conversationally fluent in Spanish. I have been learning Polish, Russian, Ukrainian, Czech, Slovak, Greek, Hungarian on my own since 2019. I spent my third year of university in Paris, studying at Université Paris I and III, and L’école Nationale Supérieure des Beaux-Arts.

My work in an informal reading group at the Harvard Kennedy School -- entitled "Algorithmic Transparency” -- informed my research practice related to the design choices of algorithm implementation in software engineering.

Utilizing traditional media and processes -- notably woodcut and observational drawing -- as a point of entry into the structure, power and poetry of algorithms, my artistic process reimagines architecture and human anatomy in the context of the instructional format of algorithms.
